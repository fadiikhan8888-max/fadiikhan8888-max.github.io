Percentage Formulas

X% of Y:
Result = (X ÷ 100) × Y

X is what % of Y:
Result = (X ÷ Y) × 100

% Change:
Result = ((New - Old) ÷ Old) × 100

Increase by %:
Result = X × (1 + Y/100)

Decrease by %:
Result = X × (1 - Y/100)

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate 20% of 500?
Use the "X% of Y" mode. Enter 20 in the percentage field and 500 in the number field. The answer is 100. Formula: (20 ÷ 100) × 500 = 100.
How do I find what percentage one number is of another?
Use the "X is what % of Y" mode. For example, 50 is what % of 200? Enter 50 and 200 — the answer is 25%. Formula: (50 ÷ 200) × 100 = 25%.
How to calculate percentage increase?
Use the "% Change" mode. Enter the old value and new value. For example, from 100 to 150 is a 50% increase. Formula: ((150 - 100) ÷ 100) × 100 = 50%.
How to calculate a discount?
Use the "X% of Y" mode to find the discount amount, or use "% Increase/Decrease" to find the final price. For a 30% discount on Rs 1000: the discount is Rs 300, and the final price is Rs 700.
What is percentage change formula?
Percentage Change = ((New Value - Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100. A positive result means increase, a negative result means decrease.

Yes, completely free with no registration needed. Use all 4 calculation modes as many times as you want — percentage of a number, percentage change, increase and decrease.
