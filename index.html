How EMI is Calculated

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It's a fixed payment you make to the lender every month until the loan is fully paid off.

EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ
─────────────────
      (1+r)ⁿ − 1

P = Principal Amount
r = Monthly Interest Rate
n = Number of Months

Our calculator uses this exact formula to give you the most accurate results.

Money-Saving Tips

  • A higher down payment reduces your EMI significantly
  • Even 0.5% lower interest rate saves thousands over time
  • Shorter tenure = more EMI but less total interest paid
  • Prepaying even once a year reduces loan duration
  • Compare rates from at least 3 lenders before deciding

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an EMI calculator?
An EMI calculator is a free online tool that helps you calculate the Equated Monthly Installment for any loan. You enter the loan amount, interest rate, and tenure — and it instantly shows your monthly payment and total interest.
How accurate is this loan EMI calculator?
Our calculator uses the standard EMI formula used by all banks and financial institutions worldwide. The results are 100% accurate for fixed-rate loans. For floating rate loans, your actual EMI may vary.
Can I use this for a home loan calculation?
Yes! Simply select the "Home Loan" tab, enter your loan amount, the bank's interest rate, and your preferred tenure (up to 30 years). The calculator will show your monthly EMI and total interest payable.
What's the difference between EMI and monthly installment?
EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed amount paid every month. It includes both the principal repayment and the interest. The ratio of principal to interest changes each month, but the total EMI stays the same.
How can I reduce my loan EMI?
You can reduce your EMI by: choosing a longer repayment tenure, making a larger down payment, negotiating a lower interest rate with your lender, or making partial prepayments to reduce the outstanding principal.
Is this EMI calculator free to use?
Yes, this EMI calculator is completely free to use with no registration required. You can calculate as many loan scenarios as you want and compare different options to find the best loan for your needs.
